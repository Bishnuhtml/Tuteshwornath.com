Geography of Bardibas and Tuteshwor Nath Temple

Location

The Tuteshwor Nath Temple is located in Bardibas, a prominent town in Province No. 2, Nepal. Bardibas is strategically positioned in the southeastern part of the country, near the border with India, making it accessible for visitors from both Nepal and neighboring regions.

Topography

The town of Bardibas is characterized by its flat and fertile plains, typical of the Terai region. The landscape features gentle undulations and is marked by lush green fields and numerous water bodies, including rivers and ponds. This topography supports the region’s agriculture and scenic beauty.

Climate

Bardibas experiences a subtropical climate with distinct seasons:

  • Summer: Hot and humid, with temperatures often exceeding 35°C (95°F) and frequent monsoon showers.
  • Monsoon: Heavy rainfall from June to September, essential for agriculture and local water resources.
  • Winter: Mild and pleasant, with temperatures ranging from 10°C to 20°C (50°F to 68°F), ideal for visiting.
  • Spring and Autumn: Moderate temperatures and lower humidity, making these seasons favorable for outdoor activities.

Vegetation and Wildlife

The area surrounding Bardibas is rich in vegetation, with a diverse range of tropical and subtropical plants. Agricultural crops such as rice, wheat, and sugarcane are prevalent. Local wildlife includes various birds, small mammals, and insects, though larger wildlife is less common in this region.
